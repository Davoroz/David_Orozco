Research

My research combines econometric rigor with data engineering and analytical tools to address issues in health economics, development, and macroeconomic policy. I have worked on projects spanning online pricing, inflation expectations, and healthcare data analytics.

Selected Research Projects

Price Rigidities and High-Frequency Data

As part of a research visit at Dalhousie University under the Emerging Leaders in the Americas Program, I studied price rigidities using high-frequency datasets, applying macroeconomic frameworks and statistical models for empirical analysis.

Health Economics and Data Analytics

At ICESI University, I contributed to health economics projects using administrative and healthcare datasets. I built automated pipelines and anomaly-detection tools in Python and R to analyze over 150,000 monthly observations, improving healthcare data quality and research efficiency.

Macroeconomic Research at the Central Bank of Colombia

Supported the development of an innovative indicator of inflation perceptions using large-scale social media data. This included processing over 80 million tweets and applying natural language processing and machine learning for sentiment analysis in a policy context.

Agricultural Price Forecasting

At Finagro, I developed econometric models (ARIMA, ARCH, GARCH) to forecast agricultural commodity prices and support evidence-based financial decisions and reporting for high-level policy evaluation.

Research Methods & Tools

  • Econometric modeling and forecasting
  • Micro and macro-level data analysis
  • Statistical programming in R, Python, and Stata
  • Natural language processing and machine learning
  • Web scraping and automated data collection
